Limits Of The Universe

By Syed Noorulla Sha Quadri Article 2 of 7
"The most beautiful thing we can experience is the mysterious. It is the source of all true art and science."
— Albert Einstein, The World as I See It, 1931

Can we ever know what the universe really is? And will the laws of the cosmos ever allow us to know that? The universe seems to have set limits to our understanding of it — meaning that we can know the universe up to a certain point, and cannot go beyond it.

Consider the speed of light, which takes 2.9 million years to reach the nearby Andromeda Galaxy. This makes us feel like even light takes enormous amounts of time to travel. But according to Einstein's special theory of relativity, something traveling at the speed of light cannot feel the passage of time. The photons themselves live in a timeless world — for a photon, the past, present, and future do not exist.

So technically, light reaches every part of the universe instantly — from its own frame of reference. This could also mean that anything capable of traveling at the speed of light becomes, in a sense, immortal. But the universe will never allow us to reach that speed.

From Einstein's special theory of relativity: the faster you move, the heavier you become — and the heaviness reduces your speed further. This is because of the increase in relativistic mass. As an object's resistance to acceleration (inertia) increases, its speed approaches — but never reaches — the speed of light. This happens because of the relationship between energy and mass described in E = MC².

The moving object has something called relativistic energy, which is interchangeable with relativistic mass. As an object gains kinetic energy, that energy contributes to its effective inertia, making it harder and harder to accelerate. The universe has baked in a cosmic speed limit — and it is not negotiable.

"The universe is under no obligation to make sense to you."
— Neil deGrasse Tyson

It's not that you aren't special — rather, you are more unique than you could have ever dreamed. You, yourself, are an eternal, essential component of the world. The limits the universe places on our understanding do not diminish us; they are an invitation to keep wondering.
