About

Who I am

I'm Syed Noorulla Sha Quadri, a BSc Physics student at Alliance University, Bangalore (CGPA 8.3), working through electrodynamics, astrophysics, and the mathematics that ties them together.

My ongoing research, Beyond Temporal Flow, reinterprets time as a relative state function — weaving together thermodynamics, special relativity, and philosophy of time. I designed a relativistic two-system thought experiment to show that time dilation might emerge from differences in physical states rather than absolute temporal intervals.

Outside research I've written 40+ articles on Medium, tutored high-school students in physics and maths, and published a 40-page book on classical mechanics, relativity, and the philosophy of time on Amazon Kindle. I've attended seminars at IISc, MPS Germany, and workshops on Einstein's theories and soft matter physics.

I'm also building computational physics projects in Python — simulating gravitational slingshots and electromagnetic wave propagation via the FDTD method.
